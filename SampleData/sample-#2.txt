SECTION 2: DARK STORE NETWORKS & COMPUTE INFRASTRUCTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The automated routing team successfully migrated its dark store location-prediction and 
inventory mapping nodes onto a localized Linux-based cluster environment.

Hardware Ingestion Limitations & Thresholds:
Each individual dark store telemetry unit features an on-site edge machine bounded 
to a hard 4.0 GB VRAM limit for handling localized order-routing queues.

• Stable Node Profile: The primary routing agent running the 3B parameter spatial-
  mapping model executes comfortably at exactly 2.3 GB VRAM. This leaves 1.7 GB 
  of physical VRAM clear to handle sudden real-time order surges without thread blocks.
  
• OOM Engine Crash: In an attempt to lower dispatch latency, engineers loaded an un-quantized 
  7B parameter predictive inventory matrix demanding a baseline of 5.2 GB VRAM. 
  The script breached the hardware limits, resulting in immediate Out-of-Memory (OOM) 
  kernel terminations and shutting down dispatches across 47 dark stores for 4 hours.
